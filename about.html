About us

Community since 1968

Established in 1968 by the Wallingford United Methodist Church as a community outreach, WCCC maintains a supportive relationship with the church while offering a non-religious curriculum to the neighborhood and greater Seattle area.

Executive Director

Paris Loutsis

Paris brings over two decades of experience in early learning and childcare, with 15 years in administrative leadership roles. She earned her Early Childhood Education degree in 2008 and was chosen as commencement speaker for her graduating class of approximately 2,000 students, appearing alongside Washington's former governor.

“Each family is a child's first teacher.”

Paris emphasizes blending home cultures with developmentally appropriate activities to help children develop self-esteem, friendship, a love of learning, and an appreciation of diversity.

Outside of work, she enjoys painting rocks as refrigerator magnets, building charcuterie platters, karaoke, and time with family. She lives in Shoreline with her 19-year-old son, who is completing his HVAC certification, and loves international cuisines and science-fiction films like Interstellar.

Our values

Support for Black Lives Matter & anti-racism

The board affirms its commitment to an inclusive, open, and supportive childcare center grounded in an anti-racist vision.

  • Monthly anti-racism discussions
  • Anti-racist educational resources for our community
  • Staff support for activism
  • Integration of anti-racist training
