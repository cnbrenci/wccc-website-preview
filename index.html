Welcome

A neighborhood center rooted in community

The Wallingford Childcare Center was established in 1968 by the Wallingford United Methodist Church as a community outreach. We maintain a supportive relationship with the church while offering a non-religious curriculum to the Wallingford neighborhood and the greater Seattle area.

We design environments where children engage with ideas, materials, and peers that reflect their interests and emerging skills — because we believe learning begins with curiosity.

Our Commitments

How we care for every child

Our work is grounded in a few promises we make to each child and family who joins us.

🌱

A positive self-image

We support intellectual, social, emotional, and physical development so children grow a strong, confident sense of who they are.

🔍

Emergent curriculum

Learning follows children's interests. Educators observe, listen, and shape experiences around each child's curiosity.

🌍

Cultural variety

We introduce a richness of cultures to promote understanding, belonging, and appreciation of our differences.

💛

Constructive emotions

We help children name and manage big feelings in healthy, constructive ways.

🤝

Family partnership

Strong staff–parent–family partnerships, with open access to classrooms throughout the day.

🎨

Room to create

Art, sensory play, and hands-on exploration are woven through every day in every classroom.

Curriculum

Curiosity leads the way

At WCCC, emergent curriculum means learning grows from children's interests at every age.

Whether it's an infant exploring textures, a toddler testing independence, or a preschooler investigating the natural world, our educators listen, observe, and design experiences that honor each child's curiosity and capability.
